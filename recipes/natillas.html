Natillas

Natillas is a Spanish dessert made with milk, egg yolks, cornstarch, sugar, cinnamon sticks, and lemon peel. To make it, simmer the milk with cinnamon sticks and lemon peel, then whisk together egg yolks, cornstarch, and sugar. Slowly add the hot milk to the egg mixture, then cook over medium heat until it thickens. Remove the cinnamon sticks and lemon peel, transfer to serving dishes, and chill for at least 2 hours before serving. Sprinkle with ground cinnamon before serving, if desired.

Ingredients

  • Milk
  • Egg yolks
  • cornstarch
  • Granulated sugar
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Lemon peel

Steps to follow:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, cinnamon sticks, and lemon peel, and bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, cornstarch, and sugar until well combined.
  3. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture while whisking continuously.
  4. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of the spoon.
  5. Remove the cinnamon sticks and lemon peel from the mixture.
  6. Pour the Natillas into individual serving dishes and allow them to cool to room temperature.
  7. Cover the dishes with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until set.
  8. Sprinkle ground cinnamon on top of the Natillas before serving, if desired.
