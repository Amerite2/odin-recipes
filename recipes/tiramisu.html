Tiramisu

Tiramisu is an Italian dessert made with ladyfingers, strong coffee, mascarpone cheese, sugar, eggs, heavy cream, and cocoa powder. To make it, dip ladyfingers in coffee and layer them in a dish. Combine whipped egg yolks, sugar, and mascarpone cheese, and fold in whipped egg whites. Spread the mixture over the ladyfingers, repeat layers, and dust the top with cocoa powder. Refrigerate and serve chilled.

Ingredients:

  • ladyfingers
  • Strong brewed coffe or espresso
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Granulated sugar
  • eggs
  • Heavy cream
  • Cocoa powder

Steps to follow:

  1. Brew strong coffee or espresso and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Separate the eggs into yolks and whites.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat the egg yolks with granulated sugar until the mixture becomes thick and pale in color.
  4. Add the mascarpone cheese to the egg yolk mixture and mix until well combined.
  5. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  6. In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms soft peaks.
  7. Gently fold the whipped egg whites into the mascarpone mixture, followed by the whipped cream.
  8. Dip the ladyfingers into the cooled coffee or espresso and place them in a single layer in a 9x13 inch dish.
  9. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  10. Repeat with another layer of soaked ladyfingers and remaining mascarpone mixture.
  11. Cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.
  12. Dust the top of the chilled tiramisu with cocoa powder before serving.
